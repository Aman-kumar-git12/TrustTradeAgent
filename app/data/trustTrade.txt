TrustTrade

What this website basically does
TrustTrade is a business-oriented marketplace built for buying and selling business assets, surplus inventory, industrial equipment, office equipment, and other commercial items. The platform is not positioned like a normal retail e-commerce store. Instead of simple fixed-price shopping, it supports a deal-making workflow where verified users discover listings, evaluate details, send interest, negotiate with the counterparty, and then move toward payment and closure.

The website connects two main sides:
1. Sellers who want to list and manage business assets under their business identity.
2. Buyers who want to discover available assets, compare options, express interest, negotiate, and complete deals.

What is the aim of this website
The main aim of TrustTrade is to create a reliable environment for real business transactions around assets and inventory. It is designed to help companies:
- convert idle or surplus assets into revenue
- discover business inventory beyond traditional retail channels
- negotiate directly instead of depending only on static prices
- work through a more trustworthy marketplace with business context and verified identities

The product also tries to solve a trust problem. A listing on TrustTrade is not just an anonymous item card. It is connected to business identity, user profile, and operational workflows like leads, dashboards, analytics, and payment.

Core website flow
The high-level TrustTrade flow is:
1. A user registers as buyer, seller, or admin.
2. A seller creates or manages a business profile.
3. The seller posts assets under a selected business.
4. Buyers browse the marketplace and open asset details.
5. Buyers express interest or negotiate.
6. Sellers manage incoming leads from their dashboard.
7. Buyers and sellers move toward acceptance and payment.
8. Checkout and payment happen through the integrated payment flow.

Main user roles
Guest
- can explore public parts such as landing, marketplace, asset pages, and public business views
- is encouraged to register to access buyer or seller workflows

Buyer
- browses marketplace assets
- submits interest
- tracks negotiation and acceptance status
- gets seller contact visibility after acceptance in relevant flows
- completes payment through checkout or buyer-side payment actions

Seller
- manages businesses
- posts assets
- receives and manages leads
- tracks listings and analytics
- optimizes performance using dashboard data

Admin
- supervises platform activity
- manages users, support, orders, and businesses

Website scope
The website scope includes:
- buyer and seller onboarding
- business profile management
- public marketplace discovery
- asset listing creation and management
- lead and negotiation workflow
- seller analytics and customer/product insights
- buyer deal tracking
- secure checkout
- support and admin operations
- AI agent assistance for navigation and platform-specific help

Why the platform has value
TrustTrade is valuable because it combines marketplace discovery with transaction structure. Instead of only showing products, it supports operational follow-through. That means the platform is not only about visibility; it is also about helping users act on listings, negotiate, and complete business transactions.
